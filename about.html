Madhubani Paintings

The unique artistic tradition has its origin in the ancient kingdom of Mithla. Tradition states that this style of painting originated at the time of the Ramayana, when king Janak commissioned artists to do paintings at the time of marriage of his daughter Sita to Lord Rama. Since then, the women of Mithla used to decorate their houses with paintings on walls & floors on festive occasions with natural colours and during marriage ceremonies. At present this art form is identified with Madhubani district of state of Bihar, India and hence is known as Madhubani painting. Since the early seventies this art form has encompassed the medium of eco- friendly handmade paper. Devotional in theme, these paintings depict scenes from the ancient epics and legends, and are rich with vedic and tantric symbolism. They are, in fact, simplistic manifestation of the philosophical heights achieved by Indian civilisation.


Asha Jha

Born in Feb, 1968, she comes from Darbhangha in northern Bihar, a region corresponding to the ancient kingdom of Mithila. This region, bears high importance in ancient Indian history, and preserves the rich heritage of folk paintings known as Mithila, or Madhubani painting.
Since 1998, she has started working on professional lines. While remaining true to the traditional roots, she tried to introduce an intellectual colour into her paintings, thus giving them a more contemporary look. Today more than 100 women artisan are associated with her and work for the development of Madhubani painting.
She won the Bihar state Award for theme based on "Jeevan- Yatra" in Mithila painting titled for the year 2007-08. She won National Merit Certificate in Madhubani Painting for the theme based on "Lord Krishna Avtaar" for the year 2008-09. She won National Award for the theme based on "Matashya Avtaar of Lord Vishnu" in Madhubani painting for the year 2012-13 by the Government of India.
